      • repeatAtFixedRate

        public TaskDefinition.Builder<V,T> repeatAtFixedRate(long period,
                                            TimeUnit unit)
        Configures the task to repeat on a fixed rate schedule. As long as executions of the task take less time than the specified period, each subsequent invocation will be scheduled at even intervals around this period. So a period of one minute will cause tasks to be scheduled one minute after the previous one, regardless of the time needed by the previous one to execute. If one or more executions take longer than the specified period, then the next task will be run as soon as possible but the frequency will never be faster than the specified rate (so short executions will not allow them to "catch up" to the schedule -- instead, some invocations are simply skipped to keep pace).
        Parameters:
        period - the period between invocations
        unit - the time unit of period
        Returns:
        this, for method chaining
